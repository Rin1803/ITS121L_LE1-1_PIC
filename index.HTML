RED VELVET

Red Velvet is a Five-member K-pop girl group under the entertainment company SM Entertainment. This group debuted on August 1, 2014, with the single Happyness, followed by their 2nd single, Be Natural, featuring SM rookie Taeyong. Initially, the group debuted with a four-member lineup: Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, and Joy, with Yeri later joining with their first Extended Play Ice Cream Cake.

The Red Velvets music reflects their group name, Red being the Experimental side of their group, occasionally using electronic and funk tunes. At the same time, the Velvet is the R&B influence side of the group, mainly consisting of ballad-type songs. Due to their versatility in each comeback, the are also named the Queen of concepts in K-pop.

Red Velvet has an extensive discography that showcases their versatility and unique style. Starting with their debut "Happiness" in the August 2014 EP "Ice Cream Cake" in March 2015, they went on to release notable works such as "The Red," "Russian Roulette," and "Perfect Velvet." The group's discography also includes repackages like "The Perfect Red Velvet" and "The ReVe Festival: Finale," featuring tracks highlighting their musical evolution.

After the huge success of the "The ReVe Festival: Finale," they came back after almost two years to release the mini album "Queendom.", "The Reve Festival 2022" and the latest comeback, "Kill Chill" In addition to their Korean releases, Red Velvet expanded their presence with Japanese albums like "#Cookie Jar" and "SAPPY." Notable singles include "Sayonara." The group's diverse discography reflects their ongoing success and influence in K-pop.

Red Velvet is under SM Entertainment, which is one of the largest and most prominent entertainment companies in South Korea. SM Entertainment, founded by Lee Soo-man, is known for managing and promoting various successful K-pop groups and artists. Red Velvet debuted under SM Entertainment in 2014 and has since become one of the most popular girl groups in the industry.

Sm Entertainment is one of the Big 3 entertainment companies in the Korean music industry, the others being YG Entertainment and JYP Entertainment. SM Entertainment is home to several prominent K-pop groups and solo artists such as EXO, Girls Generation, SHINee, and TVXQ.
